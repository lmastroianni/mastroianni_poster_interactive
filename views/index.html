The Album

‘At Folsom Prison’ is a live album by Johnny Cash, released in May 1968. Dressed in his trademark black he performed for outlaws and prisoners. He was the first artist in music history to perform in a prison, and it remains as Cash’s most bought album.

The Story

Marshall Grant (Cash’s manager) claims that a prison performance was a way to get something out of him to release, because he did’nt like being in the studio. After several ideas were pitched Cash came to the conclusion of recording live at Folsom Prison. They went to Folsom to get Cash out of the studio, and they loved the idea of doing a nice gestures for the prisoners because they can’t get out to be entertained. As far as making money, they didn’t think it would pay enough to sell for tape, but it ended up being the biggest thing Cash ever did.
